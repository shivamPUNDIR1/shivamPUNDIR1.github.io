Slum Adoption

Mahatma Gandhi once said “Be the change you want to see in the world”.

Thus bringing a substantial change in the lives of the people in need, has always been the top motive of Teens of God. Therefore, with this positive approach, we have taken a major step towards our second flagship program, The Slum Adoption Drive, which is based on adopting slums and giving a better life to the underprivileged.

This was initiated in 2019, when our team adopted 20 families, consisting of 80 people of different age groups and hence diverse needs, in Sector-31, Gurugram. During this span of time, Teens of God has done a lot, both physically and emotionally to uplift the lives of these underprivileged people. May it be providing basic living facilities to the people in need or assuring that they’re kept under full sanitation and hygiene, Teens of God has always handled the situation in hand with full managerial skills. We’ve also made them aware of their rights and the government schemes which are meant for them by educating them with all the basic information.

Education is our foremost priority hence through this programme we ensure that every little heart in the slum, gets convenient access to our education programme where we groom them for formal education, later ensuring their enrollment into schools. During harsh winters we ensure that the families are warm and safe by providing clothes and blankets, while on festivities we share happiness with them through gifts and sweets.

In times when our country was surrounded by pandemic and was facing complete lockdown, Teens of God did not stop extending their help rather increased the beneficiaries by expanding our reach to more slums in Gurugram.

We ensured that the people stayed in their homes and that they did not travel back to their home towns by educating them regarding it and also ensuring that they would regular access to food and medicines. We provided food to more than 500 underprivileged everyday, during the harsh times.

We constantly try everything possible to make them feel comfortable and safe. Slum adoption drive initially started with 80 people and is now expanded to more than 500 people.
